🧠 Betting Concepts: EV, CLV & Signals
What is EV (Expected Value) and why does it matter? ▼
Expected Value (EV) measures the theoretical profit or loss per bet over a large sample size.

EV = (Model Win Probability × Sportsbook Payout) - (Loss Probability × Wager)

A bet with +EV means the sportsbook is offering higher odds than the true mathematical probability of the event occurring. Placing only +EV bets guarantees long-term statistical profitability regardless of short-term variance.
What is CLV (Closing Line Value)? ▼
Closing Line Value (CLV) compares the odds you placed against the final line right before first pitch. If you bet Braves at +125 and the line closes at +105, you achieved positive CLV. Beating the closing line consistently is the gold standard indicator of sharp, profitable long-term sports betting.
What do PRIME, EDGE, and SKIP signals mean? ▼
• 🔥 PRIME: Top-tier mathematical advantage (historically 57%+ hit rate on ML, EV +3.5% to +8.6%). High-confidence sweet spot.
• ⚡ EDGE: Positive expected value (+1.0% to +3.5% EV). Solid value spot worth tracking.
• ⬜ SKIP: No mathematical edge detected or line is tight. The model strongly advises passing on this market.
What is Edge %? ▼
Edge % is the difference between our model's win probability and the sportsbook's implied probability. For example, if our model projects a team at 56.0% (implied odds -127) and DraftKings prices them at +100 (50.0% implied), your mathematical edge is +6.0%.
